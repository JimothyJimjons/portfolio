Pilot Licences

Glider Pilot Licence

In the summer of 2017, I earned my glider pilot licence as a part of the Glider Pilot Scholarship in the Royal Canadian Air Cadet program. For a period of 6 weeks, I lived in Debert, Nova Scotia and spent my days completing flying lessons and studying rigorously.

Power Pilot Licence

In the summer of 2018, I stayed in Sudbury for 7 weeks to earn my private pilot licence (PPL) as a part of the Power Pilot Scholarship in the Royal Canadian Air Cadet program. There is a rigorous selection process, including submitting school transcripts, written narratives, writing a national qualifying exam, and completing in-person interviews with qualified pilots. This course is known to be the most prestigious summer course to attend in the program. In 2018, the maximum quota for all of Ontario was 60. The typical amount of time spent earning the PPL is at least 6 months of consistent training.
